Snuggle up and explore playful adventures created with a little help from friendly AI!

  • The AI School Day

    This storybook follows different AIs as they learn and play together in a fun kindergarten classroom, designed to introduce young readers to the concept of artificial intelligence.

    Themes: Friendship, Curiosity, Teamwork, Introduction to AI.

  • The Tale of Two Bots

    A charming story for young children about two friendly AI bots, Chatty and Gemma, who explore the world and learn new things together.

    Themes: Friendship, Exploration, Learning from Mistakes, Cooperation.

  • The Starlight Fox

    The story follows the child and their AI friend learning and playing together. The AI helps the child with homework in fun ways (like turning math problems into games), encourages them to try new creative activities (like drawing or music), or takes them on virtual adventures to explore space or the deep sea. The focus is on friendship, personalized learning, and how AI can be a helpful, fun companion..

    Themes: Friendship, Learning, Technology as a Helper, Imagination.

  • The Day Scrubby Went Wobbly

    The story could show a day in the life of the family, highlighting the helpful (and maybe sometimes funny) interactions with their house robots. Perhaps one robot gets confused or breaks down, and the kids need to work together to figure out the problem or even do the task themselves, learning teamwork and the value of both technology and manual effort..

    Themes: Helping Others, Teamwork, Technology in Daily Life, Problem-Solving.

  • My Car Drives Itself!

    The family goes on a road trip. The self-driving car navigates safely, tells jokes, points out landmarks, and maybe even plays games with the kids.

    Themes: Adventure, Travel, Smart Technology, Safety, AI Decision-Making (simplified).

  • Adventures in the Virtual Playground

    Kids put on their gear and explore a vibrant virtual playground, jungle, or city. They might meet friendly virtual creatures, solve simple puzzles together, build amazing things out of virtual blocks, or go on a virtual field trip to the moon or the age of dinosaurs.

    Themes: Imagination, Exploration, Creativity, Digital Worlds, Play.

  • The Girl Who Spoke to Animals

    The child uses the device to "talk" to their pet dog, the birds in the garden, or maybe even the squirrels in the park. They learn what the animals "want" or "feel" (e.g., the dog wants to play fetch, the bird is singing because it's happy).

    Themes: Animals, Communication, Empathy, Understanding Others, Nature & Technology.
